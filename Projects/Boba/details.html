boba

conceptual, game design

boba is a game concept I designed and was developing in 2019 - 2020.

the goal of the project was to create a comfy real time bubble tea shop simulation game.

core mechanics

management menu

other than the shop screen, players have an additional screen for managing their boba shop. in this phone like menu, players are able to brainstorm, update their menus, plan marketing campaigns and much more!

inspiration

the game centers around these magical words called inspirations, which are the building blocks of a recipe. these inspirations are obtainable via talking with customers or from tweety and are the key components of the player's exploration aspect.

brainstorm

players have access to 2 types of inspirations; main and sub. the player can combine these inspirations to create new recipes.

menu setup

the player then sets a menu of the recipes he has unlocked. drinks have built-in stats such as base interest, base cost and tags. these values determine the demand and appeal of your menu, allowing you to attract different customers.

restocking

drinks are limited everyday (day? more information in the next section!). players need to restock their drinks, ideally based on how well they sell. for convenience, players are given the option to auto-restock since cruncing numbers isn't for everybody.

corest mechanics

wait where's the actual selling?

selling is the corest mechanic of this management game and it has a lot of aspects so I've given it it's own section.

Day & AFK System

Boba runs in real time with day cycles (like animal crossing), with an opening and closing time set by the players. During the opening hours, changes to the menu and meta aspect of the game will be postponed until the next day.

As boba is a simulation game, the opening and closing time affects variables such as daily expenses and customer generation values. Following real hours, customers would be generated during peak hours such as evenings and nights. The type of customers is also affected by these hours! School kids won't appear during weekday mornings (no hookying!).

Take note of these aspects when setting up your menu and opening time! Players need to plan carefully to maximise profits. Can't be bothered? No worries! Boba is a comfy game, you can sell just plain ol tea and customers will still appear (wow).


What happens when I don't log in? Boba's AFK system will attempt to generate these numbers using a more simplified flatter formula. Profits will still rake in even if you're not logged in! Unless you're out of stock...

Appeal & Customers

So you've set up your menu. Why are your customers only office workers??? Both customers and drinks have a tag system. For instance, the office worker trope enjoys drinks with the tag "Coffee" and "Relaxing". Setting up drinks with these tags will greatly increase the appeal of your shop to the office workers.

Setting up a diverse menu is key to gaining a wider audience. Gaining a wider audience can help unlock inspiration faster!

"But I like my office worker collection!" That's okay too! Play the game as you want as long as you enjoy!

Additional game mechanics in 'Into the Holoverse' encompass resting, progression, and missions.
